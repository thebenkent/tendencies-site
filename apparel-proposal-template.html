Decoration
Methods.

The right decoration method depends on your garment, artwork, and end use. We'll recommend the best approach for each piece in your program.

01
Embroidery

Premium and durable. Ideal for logos on polos, caps, jackets, and fleece. Available in standard or 3D puff.

Best for: Caps, polos, jackets
02
Screen Print

Cost-effective for volume runs. Bold, vibrant colour on tees, hoodies, and flat garments with spot colour artwork.

Best for: Tees, hoodies, large runs
03
Heat Transfer

Flexible for small runs or complex artwork. Includes DTF (direct to film) for photographic-quality results on any fabric.

Best for: Small runs, complex art
04
Sublimation

Full all-over print. Permanent and fade-resistant. Requires polyester-based fabrics — ideal for sportswear and teamwear.

Best for: Sportswear, all-over print
05
Woven Labels & Patches

Elevated brand detail. Woven labels, rubber patches, and badge applications for a retail-quality finish on premium garments.

Best for: Premium, retail-style kits

Indicative timelines — actual lead times vary by decoration method, garment availability, and order volume. We'll confirm specifics once quantities and artwork are finalised.

Week 1–2
Design approval

Artwork supplied or developed. Colour separations, logo clean-up, and placement sign-off. Digital mockups provided for approval.

Week 3
Sampling

Physical sample or pre-production strike-off for sign-off. Optional for high-volume orders.

Week 4–8
Production

Garment procurement, decoration, quality checking, and packing. Timeline varies by method and quantity.

Week 8–9
Delivery

Nationwide NZ courier delivery to a single address, or distributed direct to team members. Collection also available.
